## How We Create Our Content

Transparency matters to us. Here is exactly how articles on Sax & The City are created.

## AI-Assisted Writing

Our articles are generated with the help of AI (Anthropic's Claude). We use AI as a writing tool to produce well-structured, consistent content at a regular pace. We are upfront about this because we believe readers deserve to know how content is made.

## Quality Standards

Every article is built on established saxophone pedagogy and references real, verifiable sources:

- **Real musicians and recordings** -- We name specific players, albums, and tracks you can listen to.
- **Real gear** -- When we mention saxophones, mouthpieces, or reeds, we reference actual products with real model numbers.
- **Proven method books** -- Our practice advice draws from respected educational materials like Larry Teal's "The Art of Saxophone Playing" and Sigurd Rascher's "Top Tones for the Saxophone."
- **Authoritative sources** -- Each article includes a "Further Reading & Sources" section with real resources you can verify.

## What We Do NOT Do

- We do not fabricate quotes from musicians.
- We do not invent product names or model numbers.
- We do not present AI-generated opinions as expert endorsements.
- We do not use misleading bylines -- our author is "Sax & The City," not a fictional person.

## Review Process

Articles are reviewed for:

1. **Musical accuracy** -- Are the techniques, theory, and recommendations correct?
2. **Source verification** -- Are the referenced musicians, recordings, and books real?
3. **Practical value** -- Will a reader learn something actionable from this article?

## Updates and Corrections

If you spot an error or have a correction, please [contact us](/sax-and-the-city/contact/). We take accuracy seriously and will update articles when needed.

## Why AI?

AI allows us to publish helpful saxophone content regularly and consistently. Combined with editorial review and real musical knowledge, it helps us serve saxophone learners who might not have access to a private teacher. We see AI as a tool -- like a metronome or a tuner -- that helps us do our job better.
